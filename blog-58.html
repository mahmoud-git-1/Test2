Dark Souls 3 serves as the culmination of the Dark Souls trilogy, bringing together elements from both previous games while introducing new concepts and characters. The game takes place in the kingdom of Lothric, where the cycle of Fire and Dark has been repeated countless times, and the world is finally reaching its end. This is the story of the Ashen One and the final choice that will determine the fate of the world.

üî• The End of the Cycle

The World in Decay:

By the time of Dark Souls 3, the cycle of Fire and Dark has been repeated so many times that the world is beginning to collapse. The First Flame is fading faster than ever before, and the Lords of Cinder, who were supposed to link the Fire, have abandoned their thrones. The world is in a state of entropy, with time and space beginning to converge and collapse.

The Convergence:

The convergence is a phenomenon where different lands and time periods begin to merge together. This is why the Ashen One can encounter areas and characters from previous games, as the boundaries between different ages are breaking down.

üë§ The Ashen One

The Unkindled:

The player character, known as the Ashen One, is an Unkindled - an Undead who attempted to link the Fire but failed. Unlike the Chosen Undead or the Bearer of the Curse, the Ashen One is not chosen by fate but by their own failure. They are resurrected by the tolling of the bell to fulfill their duty.

The Duty of the Unkindled:

The Ashen One's duty is to return the Lords of Cinder to their thrones and link the Fire once more. However, as the game progresses, it becomes clear that this duty may not be the right choice, and the Ashen One must decide whether to continue the cycle or end it forever.

üè∞ Lothric and the Firelink Shrine

The Kingdom of Lothric:

Lothric is a kingdom built around the linking of the Fire. The royal family has dedicated themselves to producing worthy heirs who can link the Fire and continue the cycle. However, the current heir, Prince Lothric, has refused to link the Fire, leading to the kingdom's decline.

The Firelink Shrine:

The Firelink Shrine serves as the central hub of Dark Souls 3, but it is different from the previous games. It is a separate dimension that exists outside of normal time and space, accessible only to the Ashen One and other Unkindled.

üëë The Lords of Cinder

Ludleth the Exiled:

The only Lord of Cinder who remains at his throne, Ludleth is a mysterious figure who claims to have linked the Fire voluntarily. He provides guidance to the Ashen One and reveals important information about the nature of the Fire and the cycle.

Aldrich, Devourer of Gods:

Once a saint who was forced to link the Fire, Aldrich became corrupted by the process and began to devour gods. He now rules over the Cathedral of the Deep, where he continues to consume divine beings and spread his corruption.

Yhorm the Giant:

A giant who once ruled over the Profaned Capital, Yhorm linked the Fire to save his people but failed. His kingdom was consumed by the Profaned Flame, and he now wanders the ruins, consumed by grief and regret.

The Abyss Watchers:

A group of warriors who dedicated themselves to fighting the Abyss, the Abyss Watchers linked the Fire to prevent the spread of darkness. However, they became corrupted by the Abyss themselves and now fight endlessly among themselves.

Prince Lothric:

The current heir to the throne of Lothric, Prince Lothric has refused to link the Fire, believing that the cycle should end. He represents the new generation's rejection of the old ways and the desire for change.

üåë The Dark and the Deep

The Deep:

The Deep is a new concept introduced in Dark Souls 3, representing a different form of darkness than the Abyss. It is associated with water, corruption, and the consumption of gods. The Deep represents the natural evolution of the Dark, showing how it has changed over countless cycles.

The Abyss:

The Abyss continues to play a role in Dark Souls 3, but it has evolved and changed. It is no longer just a place of darkness but a force that can corrupt and consume even the most powerful beings.

üî• The First Flame and the Kiln

The Dying Flame:

The First Flame is in its final stages of life, burning weaker than ever before. The cycle has been repeated so many times that the Flame can no longer sustain itself, and the world is beginning to collapse around it.

The Kiln of the First Flame:

The Kiln has changed significantly since Dark Souls 1. It is now a desolate wasteland where the remains of countless cycles have accumulated. The Soul of Cinder, the embodiment of all those who have linked the Fire, guards the final bonfire.

üëª The Soul of Cinder

The Embodiment of the Cycle:

The Soul of Cinder is the final boss of Dark Souls 3, representing the collective will of all those who have linked the Fire throughout history. It is a powerful entity that uses the techniques and abilities of previous Lords of Cinder, showing the accumulated knowledge and power of countless cycles.

The Final Battle:

The battle against the Soul of Cinder represents the final confrontation between the Ashen One and the cycle itself. It is a battle not just against a powerful enemy but against the very concept of the cycle and the duty to link the Fire.

üåÖ The Four Endings

Link the Fire:

The traditional ending where the Ashen One links the Fire, continuing the cycle. However, this ending is presented as futile, as the Flame is too weak to sustain another cycle.

The End of Fire:

The Ashen One allows the Fire to die naturally, ushering in the Age of Dark. This ending represents the acceptance of the natural order and the end of the cycle.

The Usurpation of Fire:

The Ashen One takes the Fire for themselves, becoming the Lord of Hollows. This ending represents the rejection of both Fire and Dark in favor of a new path.

The End of Fire (Secret):

A secret ending where the Ashen One allows the Fire to die but takes the Fire Keeper's hand, suggesting a new beginning beyond the cycle.

üåä The DLC: The Ringed City

The End of the World:

The Ringed City DLC takes place at the end of the world, where the Ashen One must journey to the Ringed City, the last bastion of the gods. Here, they discover the true nature of the cycle and the role of the Pygmies in the creation of the Dark Soul.

Gael and the Dark Soul:

Slave Knight Gael is the final boss of the DLC and the entire Dark Souls series. He has consumed the Dark Soul and become a powerful entity that represents the culmination of the cycle. Defeating him brings the series to its ultimate conclusion.

üíÄ The Legacy of the Series

The End of the Cycle:

Dark Souls 3 represents the end of the cycle that has been the central theme of the series. It shows how the cycle has become unsustainable and how the world is finally reaching its natural conclusion.

The Hope for Change:

Despite the bleakness of the world, Dark Souls 3 offers hope for change. The various endings suggest that there are alternatives to the cycle, and that the world can move beyond the endless repetition of Fire and Dark.

üé≠ Key Themes and Characters

The Fire Keeper:

The Fire Keeper in Dark Souls 3 is a more prominent character than in previous games. She represents the hope for a new beginning and the possibility of breaking free from the cycle.

Siegward of Catarina:

A returning character from Dark Souls 1, Siegward represents the continuity of the series and the enduring nature of friendship and duty.

Anri of Astora:

Anri represents the new generation's struggle with the cycle and their desire to find their own path. Their story shows how the cycle affects ordinary people and the choices they must make.

üåÖ The Final Message

The End of an Era:

Dark Souls 3 marks the end of an era in gaming, bringing the Dark Souls trilogy to its conclusion. It is a game about endings, about the inevitability of change, and about the hope that comes with new beginnings.

The Legacy:

The Dark Souls series has left an indelible mark on gaming, influencing countless other games and creating a new genre of challenging, atmospheric action RPGs. Its legacy will continue to influence game design for years to come.

Dark Souls 3 is not just the end of a series but a meditation on the nature of endings themselves. It asks us to consider what it means to end something, what it means to let go, and what it means to hope for something new. In a world consumed by darkness and decay, it offers the possibility of light and renewal, showing that even in the darkest of times, there is always the hope of a new dawn.
