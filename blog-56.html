December 19, 2024 Hardware Heaven Team
üî•

Dark Souls 1 introduced players to a world of profound darkness, where the Age of Fire was dying and the Age of Dark was beginning. The game's narrative is told through environmental storytelling, cryptic dialogue, and item descriptions, creating one of gaming's most immersive and mysterious worlds. This is the story of the Chosen Undead and their journey through Lordran.

üåÖ The Age of Fire

The Beginning of Everything:

In the beginning, there was only fog, dragons, and the Everlasting Dragons who ruled the world. Then, from the Dark, came the First Flame, bringing with it disparity: light and dark, life and death, heat and cold. Four beings found the Lord Souls within the flame:

  • Gwyn, Lord of Sunlight: The Lord Soul of Light
  • The Witch of Izalith: The Lord Soul of Life
  • Nito, First of the Dead: The Lord Soul of Death
  • The Furtive Pygmy: The Dark Soul, hidden and forgotten

‚öîÔ∏è The War Against the Dragons

The Great War:

With their newfound power, the four Lords waged war against the Everlasting Dragons. Gwyn's lightning bolts shattered their stone scales, the Witch of Izalith's fire sorceries burned them, and Nito's miasma of death and disease corrupted them. The Furtive Pygmy, meanwhile, created humanity from the Dark Soul, giving birth to the human race.

The Age of Fire Begins:

After the dragons were defeated, Gwyn established the Age of Fire, becoming the Lord of Sunlight and ruling over Lordran. The other Lords established their own domains: Izalith became a city of fire sorcery, and Nito ruled over the dead. Humanity, however, was cursed with the Darksign, marking them as Undead.

üî• The Fading of the Flame

The First Sin:

As the First Flame began to fade, Gwyn faced a terrible choice. To prevent the Age of Dark from beginning, he sacrificed himself to the First Flame, becoming the first Lord of Cinder. This act, known as the First Sin, created the Curse of the Undead and bound the fate of the world to the cycle of Fire and Dark.

The Undead Curse:

Those marked with the Darksign become Undead, unable to die permanently. When they die, they return to life at bonfires, but each death brings them closer to becoming Hollow - losing their humanity and becoming mindless, aggressive creatures. The Undead are drawn to Lordran by a prophecy that speaks of a Chosen Undead who will either link the Fire or usher in the Age of Dark.

üë§ The Chosen Undead's Journey

The Asylum:

The game begins with the player character, the Chosen Undead, imprisoned in the Northern Undead Asylum. A mysterious knight drops a key, allowing the Chosen Undead to escape. After defeating the Asylum Demon, the Chosen Undead is transported to Lordran by a giant crow.

Firelink Shrine:

The journey begins at Firelink Shrine, where the Chosen Undead meets various NPCs who provide guidance and lore. The Crestfallen Warrior explains the basic premise: ring the two Bells of Awakening to open the path to Anor Londo and the Lordvessel.

üîî The Bells of Awakening

The First Bell - Undead Parish:

The Chosen Undead must travel through the Undead Burg, fighting Hollows and the Taurus Demon, before reaching the Undead Parish. Here, they face the Bell Gargoyles, two stone guardians of the first bell. Defeating them allows the Chosen Undead to ring the first bell, its toll echoing across Lordran.

The Second Bell - Blighttown:

The second bell is located deep in Blighttown, a toxic swamp at the bottom of the world. The Chosen Undead must navigate through the Depths, fighting the Gaping Dragon, before descending into Blighttown. Here, they face the Chaos Witch Quelaag, a half-spider, half-woman creature who guards the second bell.

üè∞ Anor Londo and the Lordvessel

The City of the Gods:

After ringing both bells, the Chosen Undead is transported to Anor Londo by demons. This magnificent city, bathed in eternal sunlight, is the capital of Gwyn's kingdom. Here, the Chosen Undead faces the deadly duo of Ornstein and Smough, two of Gwyn's most powerful knights.

The Lordvessel:

Defeating Ornstein and Smough grants access to Gwynevere, Princess of Sunlight, who gives the Chosen Undead the Lordvessel. This artifact is the key to accessing the Kiln of the First Flame, where the final choice must be made.

üëë The Four Lords

Seath the Scaleless:

Once an ally of the dragons, Seath betrayed them during the war and was granted a fragment of Gwyn's Lord Soul. Now insane and obsessed with achieving immortality, he rules over the Duke's Archives and the Crystal Caves, conducting horrific experiments on humans.

The Four Kings:

In the Abyss of New Londo, the Four Kings were once Gwyn's most trusted lieutenants. However, they were corrupted by the Dark and now rule over the flooded city, their souls consumed by the Abyss.

Nito, First of the Dead:

The Gravelord Nito rules over the Catacombs and the Tomb of the Giants, commanding legions of the undead. His domain is a place of death and decay, where the living are not welcome.

The Bed of Chaos:

In the depths of Lost Izalith, the Witch of Izalith attempted to recreate the First Flame but failed catastrophically. Her experiment created the Bed of Chaos, a twisted, tree-like entity that spreads chaos and corruption throughout the land.

üî• The Final Choice

The Kiln of the First Flame:

After collecting the souls of the Four Lords, the Chosen Undead can access the Kiln of the First Flame. Here, they face Gwyn, Lord of Cinder, who has been reduced to a hollow shell of his former self, endlessly linking the Fire to prevent the Age of Dark.

Two Endings:

  • Link the Fire: The Chosen Undead sacrifices themselves to the First Flame, extending the Age of Fire and becoming the next Lord of Cinder. This is the "good" ending, but it perpetuates the cycle.
  • Walk Away: The Chosen Undead leaves the First Flame to die, ushering in the Age of Dark. This is the "dark" ending, where humanity finally gets its chance to rule.

üåë The Age of Dark

The True Ending:

If the Chosen Undead chooses not to link the Fire, the First Flame finally dies, and the Age of Dark begins. This is the natural order of things, as the Dark Soul was meant to rule after the Fire faded. The Age of Dark represents humanity's time to shine, free from the influence of the gods.

üé≠ Key Characters and Their Fates

Solaire of Astora:

The jolly sun-worshipping knight who seeks his own sun. Depending on the player's actions, he can either be saved from going Hollow or become a tragic figure consumed by the Sunlight Maggot.

Artorias the Abysswalker:

Gwyn's most trusted knight, who was corrupted by the Abyss while trying to save Oolacile. His story is expanded in the Artorias of the Abyss DLC, showing his fall from grace.

Gwyndolin:

The youngest son of Gwyn, who was raised as a daughter due to his affinity for the moon. He rules over Anor Londo in secret, maintaining the illusion of the city's grandeur.

üíÄ The Cycle Continues

The Inevitable Cycle:

Dark Souls 1 establishes the fundamental cycle that governs the world: Fire rises, Fire fades, and Dark takes its place. This cycle is inevitable and cannot be broken, only delayed. The choice to link the Fire or let it die is ultimately meaningless, as the cycle will continue regardless.

Legacy:

Dark Souls 1's story is one of inevitability, sacrifice, and the futility of trying to prevent the natural order of things. It asks profound questions about the nature of existence, the cost of immortality, and whether it's better to accept change or fight against it. The game's ambiguous storytelling and multiple interpretations have made it one of the most discussed and analyzed games in history.

The world of Dark Souls is one where hope and despair walk hand in hand, where every victory is tinged with tragedy, and where the greatest enemy is not the monsters that lurk in the darkness, but the cycle of Fire and Dark that binds all existence. The Chosen Undead's journey is not just about saving the world, but about understanding the true nature of the curse that plagues humanity and the price of defying the natural order.
