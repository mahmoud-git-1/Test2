💀 Dark Souls 2: The Curse of the Undead and the Bearer of the Curse

December 19, 2024 Hardware Heaven Team
💀

Dark Souls 2 takes place in the kingdom of Drangleic, a land far removed from Lordran in both time and space. The game explores the cyclical nature of the curse, the fading of memory, and the desperate search for a cure to the Undead Curse. This is the story of the Bearer of the Curse and their journey through a world where the past and present blur together.

🌊 The Land of Drangleic

A Kingdom Built on Ruins:

Drangleic is a kingdom that has risen and fallen many times throughout history. Built upon the ruins of countless previous civilizations, it is a land where the boundaries between past and present are blurred. The kingdom was once ruled by King Vendrick, a powerful monarch who sought to break the cycle of Fire and Dark.

The Curse Spreads:

The Undead Curse has spread far beyond Lordran, affecting the entire world. In Drangleic, the curse manifests as a gradual loss of memory and humanity, driving those afflicted to seek a cure at any cost. The Bearer of the Curse is drawn to Drangleic by rumors of a way to break the curse forever.

👤 The Bearer of the Curse

The Protagonist's Journey:

The player character, known as the Bearer of the Curse, is an Undead who has lost their memory and is drawn to Drangleic by a mysterious force. Unlike the Chosen Undead, the Bearer of the Curse is not chosen by fate but by their own desperation to find a cure for the curse.

The Memory Loss:

As the game progresses, the Bearer of the Curse gradually loses their memory, forgetting their past and even their own name. This loss of identity is a central theme of the game, representing the dehumanizing effect of the curse.

🏰 Majula and the Emerald Herald

The Central Hub:

Majula serves as the central hub of Dark Souls 2, a peaceful seaside town that stands in stark contrast to the dangerous lands beyond. Here, the Bearer of the Curse meets various NPCs who provide guidance and services.

The Emerald Herald:

The Emerald Herald, also known as Shanalotte, is a mysterious figure who guides the Bearer of the Curse on their journey. She is a creation of the ancient dragons, designed to help Undead break the cycle of Fire and Dark. Her true nature and motivations remain ambiguous throughout the game.

👑 King Vendrick and the Fall of Drangleic

The Great King:

King Vendrick was once a powerful and wise ruler who sought to break the cycle of Fire and Dark. He built Drangleic into a great kingdom and gathered powerful souls to fuel his experiments. However, his quest for a cure to the curse ultimately led to his downfall.

The Fall:

Vendrick's experiments with the curse and the Dark Soul led to the corruption of his kingdom. The curse spread throughout Drangleic, turning its citizens into Hollows and driving the king into hiding. By the time the Bearer of the Curse arrives, Drangleic is a shadow of its former self.

🔥 The Four Great Souls

The Old Iron King:

Once a powerful ruler of the Iron Keep, the Old Iron King was corrupted by the power of the Old Iron Soul. His kingdom was consumed by lava, and he became a demonic entity that rules over the molten ruins of his former domain.

The Lost Sinner:

Imprisoned in the Lost Bastille, the Lost Sinner is a figure who attempted to relight the First Flame but failed. Her failure brought disaster to her kingdom, and she now languishes in eternal punishment for her hubris.

The Rotten:

A massive, grotesque entity that rules over the Black Gulch, the Rotten is composed of countless corpses and body parts. It represents the accumulation of death and decay that comes with the curse.

The Duke's Dear Freja:

A massive spider that has consumed the soul of the Duke of Tseldora, Freja represents the corruption of knowledge and the dangers of seeking forbidden wisdom.

🌑 The Dark and the Abyss

The Abyss Watchers:

Throughout Drangleic, the Bearer of the Curse encounters various manifestations of the Dark and the Abyss. These entities represent the corruption that comes with the curse and the inevitable decay of all things.

The Darklurker:

A mysterious entity that dwells in the Abyss, the Darklurker represents the true nature of the Dark. Defeating it grants access to powerful Dark magic and reveals the true nature of the curse.

👻 The Memory of the Ancients

The Memory Fragments:

Throughout the game, the Bearer of the Curse can access memory fragments that reveal the history of Drangleic and the events that led to its fall. These memories show the kingdom in its prime and the tragic events that brought about its destruction.

The Ancient Dragon:

In the Dragon Shrine, the Bearer of the Curse encounters an Ancient Dragon, one of the last of its kind. This dragon holds the key to understanding the true nature of the curse and the cycle of Fire and Dark.

🔥 The Throne of Want

The Final Destination:

The Throne of Want is the ultimate destination of the Bearer of the Curse's journey. It is here that they must make the final choice: to take the throne and become the next monarch of Drangleic, or to walk away and seek a different path.

The Choice:

Taking the throne means accepting the responsibility of ruling Drangleic and continuing the cycle of Fire and Dark. Walking away means rejecting the throne and seeking a different solution to the curse.

🌊 The DLC: The Crown Trilogy

The Crown of the Sunken King:

This DLC takes the Bearer of the Curse to the sunken city of Shulva, where they must navigate a complex puzzle-filled environment to reach the Crown of the Sunken King. The crown holds the power to resist the curse.

The Crown of the Old Iron King:

In the Brume Tower, the Bearer of the Curse must face the remnants of the Old Iron King's kingdom and claim the Crown of the Old Iron King. This crown grants the power to resist the curse.

The Crown of the Ivory King:

The final DLC takes place in the frozen city of Eleum Loyce, where the Bearer of the Curse must face the Ivory King and claim the final crown. With all three crowns, the Bearer of the Curse can break the cycle of Fire and Dark.

💀 The True Ending

Breaking the Cycle:

With all three crowns, the Bearer of the Curse can achieve the true ending of Dark Souls 2. By wearing the crowns, they become immune to the curse and can break the cycle of Fire and Dark. This represents the ultimate victory over the curse and the beginning of a new age.

The New Age:

The true ending suggests that the Bearer of the Curse has found a way to break the cycle that has plagued the world for countless ages. This represents hope for a future free from the curse and the endless cycle of Fire and Dark.

🎭 Key Themes and Characters

Memory and Identity:

Dark Souls 2 explores the theme of memory and identity, showing how the curse gradually erodes the self. The Bearer of the Curse's journey is as much about reclaiming their lost memories as it is about breaking the curse.

Lucatiel of Mirrah:

A tragic character who represents the human cost of the curse. As the game progresses, Lucatiel gradually loses her memory and humanity, becoming a Hollow. Her story serves as a warning of what happens to those who cannot break the curse.

Vendrick's Legacy:

King Vendrick's story represents the futility of trying to break the cycle through force and power. Despite his great strength and wisdom, he ultimately failed to find a cure for the curse, leaving his kingdom in ruins.

🌅 The Legacy of Dark Souls 2

A Different Perspective:

Dark Souls 2 offers a different perspective on the Dark Souls universe, focusing on the human cost of the curse rather than the cosmic struggle between Fire and Dark. It shows how the curse affects ordinary people and the lengths they will go to find a cure.

The Hope for Change:

Unlike Dark Souls 1, which ends with the inevitability of the cycle, Dark Souls 2 offers hope that the cycle can be broken. The true ending suggests that with the right knowledge and power, the curse can be overcome and a new age can begin.

Dark Souls 2 is a story of desperation, hope, and the human spirit's ability to overcome even the most insurmountable obstacles. It shows that even in a world consumed by darkness, there is always the possibility of finding light and breaking free from the chains that bind us.
